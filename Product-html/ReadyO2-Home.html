FAQ

  1. Is there a continuous flow oxygen concentrator?

    • Yes, there are continuous flow oxygen concentrators. These devices provide a steady stream of oxygen at a set flow rate, typically ranging from 1 to 5 liters per minute (LPM). They are ideal for individuals who need consistent oxygen therapy throughout the day. Continuous flow concentrators are often used for home oxygen therapy, delivering a reliable and uninterrupted oxygen supply for patients with respiratory conditions. Strong oxygen output is essential for maintaining proper oxygen levels, especially for those requiring higher flow rates.


  2. What is the disadvantage of a continuous flow oxygen system?

    • The main disadvantage of a continuous flow oxygen system is that it typically uses more battery power than a pulse flow oxygen concentrator. This means the device may need to be recharged more frequently, especially for portable models. Additionally, continuous flow systems are generally larger and heavier than pulse flow models, making them less convenient for travel. They also deliver oxygen continuously, even when you're not breathing, which can lead to wastage of oxygen and higher costs in some cases. These factors can make continuous flow systems less efficient for some users compared to other types of oxygen concentrators.


  3. Does Inogen 5 have continuous flow?

    • Yes, the Inogen One G5 offers both pulse flow and continuous flow settings, making it versatile for various oxygen therapy needs. However, it is primarily designed for pulse flow, which is typically used for most patients. The continuous flow option on the Inogen One G5 is available for those who need a steady, uninterrupted oxygen supply, but it's important to check if it meets the specific flow rate required for your therapy. The device provides different flow settings to accommodate a wide range of oxygen needs.


  4. Can home oxygen concentrator run continuously?

    • Yes, a home oxygen concentrator is designed to run continuously, providing a steady supply of oxygen for as long as it is needed. Most home oxygen concentrators are built to operate 24/7, ensuring a constant flow of oxygen, making them suitable for patients who require long-term oxygen therapy. However, it's important to check the manufacturer's specifications to confirm the continuous operation limits and to ensure proper maintenance for long-term use.


  5. Is Readyo2 Home FDA approved?Is it a medical device that requires a prescription?

    • Yes, the ReadyO2 Home is FDA approved, and it is classified as a medical device. As a medical-grade device, it requires a prescription for purchase. This ensures that the oxygen concentrator is used under the supervision of a healthcare professional and is suitable for the user's specific medical needs. Always consult with your doctor or medical professional before purchasing and using the device.
