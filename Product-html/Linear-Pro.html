FAQ

  1. How much does portable ultrasound cost?

    • The cost of portable ultrasound machines can vary significantly depending on the brand, features, and imaging capabilities. For **entry-level models**, prices typically range from **$2,000 to $5,000**, offering basic imaging functions suitable for home use or limited diagnostic purposes. These models may include handheld ultrasound devices with essential features for quick scans. **Mid-range machines** are priced between **$5,000 and $15,000**, providing higher-quality imaging, multiple probe compatibility, and additional diagnostic modes such as Doppler or 3D imaging. These are commonly used in clinics or small medical practices. **High-end portable ultrasound machines** can cost **$15,000 or more**, offering advanced functionalities, superior image resolution, and longer battery life, suitable for hospital or specialized medical use.


  2. What is a linear probe ultrasound used for?

    • A **linear probe ultrasound** is used primarily for imaging structures near the surface of the body. It is highly effective in providing **high-resolution images** of superficial tissues, making it ideal for several medical specialties. In **vascular imaging**, it helps assess blood vessels and diagnose conditions like peripheral artery disease, venous thrombosis, and carotid stenosis. For **musculoskeletal (MSK) imaging**, the linear probe is ideal for examining muscles, tendons, ligaments, and joints, detecting injuries, tendonitis, sprains, or bursitis. It is also commonly used in **thyroid imaging** to check for abnormalities such as cysts, nodules, or cancer. Additionally, **breast imaging** benefits from linear probes, providing clear visuals for detecting lumps, cysts, or other abnormalities. In **pediatric imaging**, it is used to examine organs or tissues close to the skin's surface, making it suitable for children. Finally, the **linear probe ultrasound** is used for **abdominal imaging**, although it is more effective in assessing structures just beneath the skin.


  3. Is it legal to own an ultrasound machine?

    • In most countries, it is **legal to own an ultrasound machine**, but there are specific regulations governing their purchase and use. Generally, these devices are regulated as medical equipment, meaning they are subject to health and safety standards. For personal or non-medical use, **restrictions may apply**, as operating ultrasound machines typically requires proper training to ensure safety and accuracy in the results. In many regions, only licensed medical professionals such as doctors, nurses, and ultrasound technicians are legally permitted to operate these machines. However, **consumer-grade ultrasound devices** for home use or educational purposes may be more accessible, but they are typically not as powerful or accurate as clinical models. It's important to consult local laws or regulations before purchasing an ultrasound machine, especially if you're planning to use it in a professional setting or medical practice.
