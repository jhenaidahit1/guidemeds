  2. What is the difference between an IV pump and an infusion pump?

    • An IV pump and an infusion pump are often used interchangeably, but there is a slight difference. An IV pump specifically refers to a device designed for delivering fluids, medications, or nutrients directly into a patient's veins through an intravenous (IV) line. It is commonly used in hospitals for precise fluid and drug delivery. On the other hand, an infusion pump is a broader term that refers to any device that delivers fluids, medications, or nutrients into the body, either intravenously, subcutaneously, or through other routes. While all IV pumps are infusion pumps, not all infusion pumps are IV pumps. In short, IV pumps are a specific type of infusion pump designed for intravenous delivery.


  3. What is a portable infusion pump?

    • A portable infusion pump is a compact, lightweight device designed to deliver fluids, medications, or nutrients into a patientâ€™s body, typically through an intravenous (IV) line, while allowing for mobility. Unlike stationary infusion pumps, portable versions can be easily carried by patients, making them ideal for home care, outpatient settings, or during travel. These pumps ensure precise and controlled fluid delivery, even outside of a clinical environment. Portable infusion pumps provide the flexibility to administer treatments while allowing patients to maintain their daily activities.


  4. How much does an infusion pump cost in dollars?

    • The cost of an infusion pump can vary widely depending on the type, features, and brand. On average, prices for infusion pumps typically range from $1,000 to $5,000 USD for basic models. However, advanced, specialized pumps (such as those for chemotherapy or specialized drug delivery) can cost upwards of $10,000 USD or more. Additionally, portable infusion pumps or smaller models for home use may cost between $600 to $3,000 USD. It's best to check with medical equipment suppliers for exact pricing, as costs can also vary based on location and available features.


  5. Does this infusion pump work with any iv sets?

    • Most infusion pumps are designed to work with specific IV sets that are calibrated and compatible with the device to ensure accurate delivery of fluids. Using an incompatible IV set may cause errors, such as incorrect flow rates or alarms. It is essential to check the manufacturer's guidelines for the infusion pump to confirm which IV sets are compatible. If the pump supports universal or standardized IV sets, it will be mentioned in the product specifications.
