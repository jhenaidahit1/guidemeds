FAQ

  1. What is a convex probe used for in ultrasound?

    • A convex probe in ultrasound is primarily used for imaging deeper structures within the body. It features a curved shape, allowing for a wider field of view and better penetration of sound waves, making it ideal for abdominal scans, obstetrics, gynecology, and urology. The convex probe provides clear images of internal organs, such as the liver, kidneys, and bladder, and is commonly used in applications requiring deeper tissue visualization due to its ability to scan at lower frequencies with a larger depth range.


  2. Are portable ultrasound machines any good?

    • Yes, portable ultrasound machines are highly effective and offer many advantages. They provide convenience, mobility, and ease of use, allowing healthcare professionals to perform scans at the point of care, whether in hospitals, clinics, or even at home. Despite their compact size, portable ultrasound machines deliver high-quality imaging, making them ideal for quick diagnostics, emergency situations, and routine check-ups. They are particularly useful in remote areas, for mobile clinics, and in specialty applications like obstetrics, gynecology, and cardiology.


  3. How much does portable ultrasound cost?

    • The cost of a portable ultrasound machine typically ranges from $1,000 to $5,000, depending on the brand, features, and specifications. Basic models with essential features may start around $1,000, while more advanced units with higher imaging quality, specialized probes, and additional functions can cost up to $5,000 or more. Factors like warranty, included accessories, and any subscription fees for software may also impact the overall price.


  4. What is the disadvantage of portable ultrasound?

    • One of the main disadvantages of **portable ultrasound machines** is that they generally offer lower image resolution compared to traditional, larger ultrasound machines. This can limit their effectiveness in some specialized diagnostic situations where high-detail imaging is crucial. Additionally, while they are more compact and lightweight, portable models may have a smaller scanning depth range, which can affect their ability to visualize deeper tissues or organs. Lastly, the battery life of some portable ultrasound machines may be limited, requiring frequent recharging during extended use.


  5. Is a Wi-Fi connection required?

    • A **Wi-Fi connection** is not always required for **portable ultrasound machines**, but it is typically needed for features such as image sharing, cloud storage, or real-time remote consultations. Many portable ultrasound models, including the **DRSONO Convex Pro**, use Wi-Fi to transfer data to smartphones, tablets, or computers for easy access and management of patient information. However, some devices may still function and display images locally without an active Wi-Fi connection.
