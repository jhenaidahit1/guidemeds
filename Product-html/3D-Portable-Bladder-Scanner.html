FAQ

  1. What exactly is measured by a bladder scanner?

    • bladder scanner measures bladder volume, determining the amount of urine present in the bladder. This is achieved through ultrasound technology, which allows for non-invasive and highly accurate assessments. By capturing real-time images, the scanner calculates the volume of fluid within the bladder, providing essential diagnostic data. The primary measurement is the bladder volume, expressed in milliliters (mL), which helps healthcare providers evaluate urinary retention or monitor bladder function. Another critical metric is the post-void residual volume, indicating how much urine remains in the bladder after urination. Advanced scanners also offer detailed bladder wall images, which can help identify structural abnormalities or irregularities. These measurements are essential for diagnosing and managing conditions like urinary retention, bladder dysfunction, or post-surgical recovery. The data aids in improving patient outcomes by minimizing invasive procedures, such as catheterization, and providing a quick, painless alternative for both patients and healthcare providers.


  2. Does the BVT01 device require any training?

    • The BVT01 Portable Bladder Scanner is designed to be highly intuitive and easy to use, which means it generally does not require extensive training. The device features a user-friendly interface with a 5.7-inch color LCD touch screen, making it simple for medical professionals to operate with little to no prior experience. However, while no formal training is required, users may benefit from referring to the user manual and instructional videos provided with the device to understand its full range of features and ensure accurate usage. For those new to bladder scanning technology, a brief orientation or demonstration could enhance their efficiency and confidence in using the device.


  3. How much does a bladder scan cost?

    • The cost of a bladder scan can vary depending on where it's performed and the type of scan used. In a clinical or hospital setting, a bladder ultrasound typically costs between $100 and $500 per scan, with additional fees for consultations or follow-up appointments. For those looking for more convenience, investing in a portable bladder scanner for home use, such as the BladGo 2.0, can be a more cost-effective solution in the long run. While the initial price of a portable scanner ranges from $3,799.00 to $4,799.00, this allows for repeated, non-invasive scans at home without additional costs per use. Insurance may cover the cost of medical bladder scans in some cases, but it's best to confirm with your provider. Though the upfront cost for home-use devices can be significant, it can save money over time, especially for individuals who need regular monitoring of bladder health.


  4. Why Are Bladder Scanners Necessary?

    • Bladder scanners are crucial for non-invasive measurement of bladder volume, aiding in the diagnosis of conditions like urinary retention and incontinence. They provide quick and accurate results without requiring catheterization, minimizing patient discomfort and the risk of infections. These devices are particularly valuable for pre- and post-operative monitoring, ensuring proper bladder function during recovery. By reducing the need for invasive procedures, bladder scanners improve patient care, protect dignity, and enhance overall healthcare efficiency. They are a cost-effective and time-saving solution for modern medical practices.


  5. Why Are Bladder Scanners Necessary?

    • A nurse would use a bladder scanner by first ensuring the patient is comfortably positioned, usually lying down with their abdomen exposed. They would then apply a small amount of ultrasound gel to the bladder area to enhance the scan's accuracy. The nurse places the handheld probe over the lower abdomen, adjusting it to capture the bladder's image. Once in position, the nurse activates the scanner, which uses ultrasound technology to measure bladder volume within seconds. Afterward, the results are displayed on the scanner's screen, showing bladder volume and helping assess any urine retention. Finally, the nurse records and reports the results, providing vital information for further medical evaluation or treatment.
