FAQ

  1. How much does portable ultrasound cost?

    • The cost of a portable ultrasound can vary widely depending on the brand, features, and imaging capabilities. Entry-level models typically range from $2,000 to $5,000, offering basic imaging functions and suitable for limited diagnostic purposes. These devices are often compact and ideal for home use or small practices. For more advanced options, mid-range models are priced between $5,000 and $15,000. These machines provide superior imaging quality, compatibility with multiple probes, and additional diagnostic modes like Doppler imaging. Finally, high-end portable ultrasound systems can cost $15,000 or more, providing the latest technology, exceptional image resolution, and extended battery life, making them suitable for hospitals or specialized medical practices.


  2. How do you use a hand held bladder scanner?

    • Using the BladGo 2.0 Handheld Bladder Scanner is simple and effective for measuring bladder volume quickly and accurately. To start, power on the device and select the appropriate patient mode (male, female, child, obesity, or female with hysterectomy) based on the patient's profile. Position the patient comfortably on their back, exposing the lower abdomen for easier access to the bladder area. You may optionally apply ultrasound gel for better sound wave transmission. Once the probe is placed on the bladder area, activate the scan by pressing the scan switch on the ergonomic probe. Hold the probe steady while the device scans, and it will display the bladder volume on the 3.5” LCD screen. You can save the data and images to a USB drive using the U-Disk feature for future reference. The device also offers two modes: Lite Mode, which is user-friendly for beginners, and Expert Mode, which is designed for healthcare professionals requiring more advanced settings. This portable scanner is ideal for non-invasive, precise bladder measurements at home or in clinical settings.


  3. Are portable ultrasound machines any good?

    • Portable ultrasound machines have become an increasingly popular choice due to their convenience, flexibility, and ability to provide high-quality imaging in a compact form. These devices are highly regarded for their portability, making them ideal for point-of-care imaging in various settings, such as at the bedside, in emergency situations, or in remote locations. Many modern portable ultrasound machines offer impressive image clarity, advanced features like Doppler imaging, and compatibility with mobile devices, allowing easy access to scan results and data sharing. They are especially valuable for small medical practices or home healthcare providers looking for affordable ultrasound options. While high-end models can offer superior imaging quality, even entry-level portable ultrasounds provide reliable diagnostics for basic procedures. As technology advances, these machines continue to prove their worth, balancing performance with portability.


  4. What is the disadvantage of portable ultrasound?

    • One of the main disadvantages of portable ultrasound machines is their limited imaging capabilities compared to larger, more advanced ultrasound systems. While portable models offer convenience and flexibility, they often have lower resolution and fewer imaging modes, which can impact their diagnostic accuracy, especially for more complex conditions. Additionally, the smaller size and lightweight design can sometimes mean fewer features or reduced depth penetration, making them less suitable for imaging deeper organs or structures. Battery life is another limitation; although many portable units offer several hours of continuous scanning, they may require frequent recharging during long or busy clinical sessions. Finally, cost can also be a concern, as the initial investment in high-quality portable ultrasound machines can still be significant, particularly when compared to traditional ultrasound systems.


  5. Which brand of ultrasound machine is best?

    • There are several high-quality brands when it comes to ultrasound machines, and the best one for you will depend on your specific needs, such as portability, image quality, and specialty use. However, one of the most reputable brands in the market is GE Healthcare. Known for its advanced imaging technology and wide range of options, GE offers ultrasound machines that deliver superb image clarity and are used across various specialties, from obstetrics to cardiology. GE’s portable models, like the Vscan Extend, provide excellent image quality in a compact design, making it ideal for point-of-care diagnostics. While other brands, such as Philips and Siemens, also offer excellent options, GE’s ultrasound machines tend to strike a balance between affordability, reliability, and advanced features for many users.
